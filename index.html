My story

The journey that
changed everything

For as long as I can remember, I knew something was different about me. Not in a way I could explain. Just a feeling that lived in my body long before I had words for it.

I spent years searching for answers in the wrong places. Googling symptoms at 3am because my body was doing things that did not feel right and doctors kept sending me home with nothing. Always exhausted. Brain on overdrive. Feeling things with an intensity that made me wonder if I was broken.

I felt like the black sheep everywhere I went. My family. My friendships. Like nobody could quite reach what was actually happening inside me. I always felt different but I could never explain why.

In my teens I found something that worked, for a while. Drugs and alcohol quieted the storm in my mind and body. But the weeks afterwards I only felt worse. Trading one kind of pain for another.

For years I lived inside systems never built for the way my mind works. Then I lost a baby. And that loss cracked me open in a way nothing else could.

That pain became the catalyst for my awakening. Through deep emotional, energetic and nervous system work, I began to understand what had actually been happening all along. Not a disorder to manage. A wound to heal. A sensitivity that, once understood, became my greatest gift.

Today, my family and I travel the world, worldschooling our children and moving at our own rhythm. This is what healing actually looks like.

My work is not about helping women manage ADHD. It is about helping them remember who they are beneath the label, the conditioning, and the exhaustion of a lifetime spent searching.
